# People Analytics: Time-to-Hire Optimization

## Business Problem

A fast-growing tech company is experiencing hiring delays, with an average Time-to-Hire of **52 days**, leading to:

- Project delays  
- Increased workload on existing teams  
- Candidate drop-off during the hiring process  
- Higher cost per hire  

---

## Objectives

This project aims to:

- Identify key factors influencing Time-to-Hire  
- Detect bottlenecks across hiring stages  
- Build a predictive model to estimate hiring duration  
- Provide actionable recommendations to reduce Time-to-Hire by at least **20% without increasing recruitment capacity**  

---

## Analytical Approach

The analysis combines **HR domain knowledge + Data Science techniques**:

### Data Analysis
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Funnel analysis (time per stage, drop-off rates)

### Feature Engineering
- Vacancy characteristics (seniority, department, remote vs onsite)
- Funnel metrics (stage duration, rejected candidates)
- Recruiter workload and experience
- Role urgency level

### Modeling
- Regression models (baseline + advanced models)
- Model evaluation (MAE, RMSE)
- Prediction of Time-to-Hire (days)

---

## Key Insights (Example)

- Certain roles (e.g., senior tech positions) significantly increase hiring time  
- Bottlenecks are concentrated in specific stages (e.g., technical interviews)  
- Recruiter workload has a measurable impact on process speed  
- High urgency roles do not always translate into faster hiring  

---

## Business Impact

Reducing Time-to-Hire has direct impact on:

- Faster project delivery  
- Lower opportunity cost  
- Improved candidate experience  
- Increased hiring efficiency  

Estimated impact of a **20% reduction** in Time-to-Hire:
- Significant cost savings per hire  
- Increased team productivity  
- Reduced talent loss
